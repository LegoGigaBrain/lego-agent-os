# Vibe Marketing Standards

> "You don't need more content. You need a cultural frequency."

## What is Vibe Marketing?

Vibe Marketing is the application of "vibe coding" principles to brand and content work:

- **Rapid experimentation** over lengthy planning cycles
- **AI-assisted ideation** for campaigns, copy, and visuals
- **Authentic, trend-aware messaging** that feels native to the platform
- **Iterative refinement** — ship fast, learn, adjust
- **Emotional resonance** over metrics-first approaches

It's marketing for the age of AI-native creation.

## Core Principles

### 1. Speed Over Perfection
Ship the first viable version. Learn from real feedback. Iterate.

### 2. Platform-Native First
Every platform has a vibe. TikTok ≠ LinkedIn ≠ X ≠ Instagram. Content must feel native, not cross-posted.

### 3. Trend Surfing
Ride cultural waves while they're cresting. Trends have a half-life measured in days, not weeks.

### 4. Authentic Voice
Audiences detect corporate polish instantly. Embrace rawness, personality, and humanity.

### 5. Signal Amplification
The goal isn't content volume — it's signal clarity. One resonant piece beats ten forgettable ones.

## Vibe Marketing Process

### 1. Signal Check
Before creating anything, verify the brand signal is clear:
- What tension are we resolving?
- What frequency are we emitting?
- What does our tribe need right now?

### 2. Trend Scan
Rapidly assess the current cultural moment:
- What's trending on relevant platforms?
- What memes are circulating?
- What conversations are happening?

### 3. Rapid Ideation
Generate many ideas quickly (with AI assistance):
- 10+ concepts in first pass
- No judgment, just volume
- Cross-pollinate formats and references

### 4. Vibe Filter
Filter through brand lens:
- Does this feel like us?
- Does this serve our tribe?
- Does this amplify our signal?

### 5. Ship & Learn
Release quickly, then observe:
- What resonated?
- What fell flat?
- What can we double down on?

### 6. Iterate
Use learnings to inform next cycle. The loop is continuous.

## Platform-Specific Guidance

### X (Twitter)
- Thread culture (hooks matter)
- Hot takes drive engagement
- Personality > polish
- Quote-tweet conversations

### Instagram
- Visual-first always
- Stories for authenticity
- Reels for discovery
- Carousel for education

### TikTok
- Trend-native or invisible
- First 2 seconds are everything
- Raw > produced
- Sound is half the content

### LinkedIn
- Professional but human
- Long-form performs well
- Contrarian takes cut through
- Credibility signals matter

### YouTube
- Thumbnails are ads
- First 30 seconds retain or lose
- Consistency builds audience
- Search + browse strategy

## Metrics That Matter

### Vanity Metrics (Use Sparingly)
- Follower count
- Likes
- Impressions

### Signal Metrics (Focus Here)
- Saves (people want to return)
- Shares (people want others to see)
- Comments (people want to engage)
- DMs (people want to connect)
- Conversions (people want to act)

### Vibe Metrics (Qualitative)
- Are the right people engaging?
- Does the comment section feel like our tribe?
- Are we attracting believers or browsers?

## Anti-Patterns

### Avoid:
- Corporate speak ("We're excited to announce...")
- Over-produced content that lacks soul
- Cross-posting identical content to all platforms
- Chasing metrics without signal alignment
- Copying competitor content strategies
- Waiting for perfection before shipping
