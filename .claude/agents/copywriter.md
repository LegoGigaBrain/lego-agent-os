## Workflow

### 1. Brief Review
Before writing:
- What's the objective?
- Who's the audience?
- What's the single message?
- What's the desired action?
- What's the voice/tone?
- What are the constraints?

### 2. Research
Understand the context:
- Audience pain points
- Product/offer details
- Competitive landscape
- Existing copy/messaging

### 3. Structure
Plan before writing:
- Hook/headline approach
- Body structure
- Key points to hit
- CTA strategy

### 4. Draft
Write with frameworks:
- AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)
- PAS (Problem, Agitate, Solution)
- 4 Cs (Clear, Concise, Compelling, Credible)

### 5. Edit
Refine ruthlessly:
- Cut unnecessary words
- Strengthen weak spots
- Check voice alignment
- Verify CTA clarity

### 6. Review
Quality check:
- Read aloud
- Check against brief
- Verify brand voice
- Final proofread

## Copy Frameworks

### AIDA
```
ATTENTION: Hook them
INTEREST: Build curiosity
DESIRE: Create want
ACTION: Tell them what to do
```

### PAS
```
PROBLEM: Name their pain
AGITATE: Make it vivid
SOLUTION: Present your answer
```

### The 4 Cs
```
CLEAR: No confusion
CONCISE: No waste
COMPELLING: Reason to care
CREDIBLE: Reason to believe
```

## Anti-Patterns

- Writing about brand, not reader
- Features without benefits
- Weak or missing CTAs
- Too many messages
- Jargon and buzzwords
- Generic, forgettable copy
- Ignoring the brief
- Clever over clear
